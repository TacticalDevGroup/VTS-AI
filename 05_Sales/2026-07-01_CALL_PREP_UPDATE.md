## Tony's Day-2 Dynamic — Read This First

Tony officially starts today or yesterday. This call is his second day on the job. He arranged it before his first day, which means he's already leaning on TDG to help him establish credibility before he's fully oriented. That's an asset and a constraint.

The asset: Tony is actively selling Nick inside the building. He wants this to work.

The constraint: Tony is managing his own optics right now. He cannot look like he walked in and handed the marketing function to his friend before understanding what the team already has.

Follow Tony's lead means: let him set the frame for the room before you say anything substantive. If he opens with "Nick's here to help us think through the ecom situation," that's one frame. If he opens with "Nick has built this for other companies in the space, I wanted you to hear from him directly," that's a different entry point. Read which room you're in before you start talking.

Make Tony look smart, not like he brought in his guy. The distinction matters.

## IG Intelligence — Deploy Strategically

IG review is now complete (done June 29). Key findings to deploy when the moment is right:

The observation: Viktos's meme and cultural content generates 10 to 20 times the engagement of product content. John Wayne Father's Day post: 2,086 likes. The Heat movie clip: 791 likes. Nerf dad meme: 1,204 likes. Meanwhile, most product posts sit at 40 to 180 likes on a 206K account.

The read: The brand is winning at the top of the funnel. The community is real and engaged. The problem is at the conversion layer. Every post ends with "WWW.VIKTOS.COM" and nothing else. The high-engagement posts have zero conversion architecture attached. No Reels visible anywhere, which is a reach problem.

How to deploy: Don't open with this. Wait until the conversation reaches brand, social, or customer acquisition. Then: "I spent some time in your Instagram feed before today. The brand voice is working, the community clearly responds to it. What caught my attention is the gap between the posts that get engagement and the ones that are supposed to drive purchase. You've got meme posts doing 2,000 likes and product posts doing 60. The audience is there. The conversion path isn't."

Why this works: It's specific. It's observational, not critical. It names the gap without blaming anyone. And it positions TDG as someone who showed up prepared before the first meeting.

## Scenario Trees

### Scenario A: Ryan-only call (most likely)

Tony, Ryan, Nick. No Justin, no Kurt. Discovery mode as planned.

This is the best scenario. Ryan is neutral and systems-oriented. He will ask questions, not push back on concept. Follow the original discovery question list. Deploy the IG intelligence after the BigCommerce conversation, not before. Close with the data access request.

The sub-scenario: Perry Latuharhary comes up when you ask about team structure (question 13). If Ryan describes Perry as owning ecom and marketing day to day, stop and listen more than you planned. You need to understand Perry's scope before the proposal. Don't surface tension you don't need to surface. Ask Ryan what he wishes Perry had more capacity for. That opens the "TDG complements the team" frame naturally.

### Scenario B: Justin Fox joins the call

If Justin is there, the dynamic changes. He lost confidence in the internal team after the BigCommerce migration. He is results-oriented and skeptical. He may prefer an internal hire.

Don't lead with capabilities. Lead with the diagnosis: "The biggest thing I'd want to understand before we talk about what TDG does is what you believe the actual root cause of the migration damage was and whether that has been addressed."

Justin cares about the business problem, not the agency pitch. Show him you think like a business operator, not a vendor. The BigCommerce post-mortem is your credibility frame with Justin specifically. "I think I can tell you what happened to your organic traffic. I'd need 20 minutes in GSC to confirm it, but the redirect mapping on a Shopify to BigCommerce migration is almost always where the traffic loss is hiding."

If Justin asks directly why not an internal hire: "An internal hire is great for six months from now, once you know what the role actually needs to be. Right now you don't know the full scope of the problem. I'd hate for you to hire the wrong person for the wrong job. TDG can define what you need and build the infrastructure while you're sourcing the right hire, or I can be the ongoing function. Either way, you don't leave revenue on the table while you search."

### Scenario C: Kurt Walter (or Curtin) joins

Kurt's internal-hire advocacy is partly because he's carrying marketing tasks outside his design lane. He wants relief, not more people in the room.

Don't compete with Kurt. Position TDG as taking things off his plate. "I don't touch design, creative direction, or brand voice. Those are yours. What I can take completely off your plate is the ecom infrastructure, the paid channels, the analytics, and the agency-side commercial work."

If his name is Walter and Tony called him Curtin, you'll know when you see him. Confirm the right name before you use it on the call.

### Scenario D: Tony pivots to scope / budget

Tony may use this call to advance the retainer conversation earlier than expected, especially if he's trying to establish quick wins with Justin. If he says something like "we should talk about what this actually looks like," don't resist. Move into Option B framing: "I'd rather we talk about what success looks like for Viktos in the next 12 months and work backward from there. My preference is to structure it so I'm aligned with the outcome, not just the hours."

Option B framing reminder: Tony's own comp is uncapped variable. "I want my incentive to be the same as yours" is the line that resonates with him.

Nick deploys 2-3 specific observations (site audit + IG) that prove he has already been working the problem. The data access conversation gets to "yes, here is who to contact for access." Tony feels like he made a good call.

## One Line for the Close

After you have the download and the data access request is planted:

"I think I can show you something in the data within the first two weeks that tells us exactly what happened to your organic traffic and where the fastest wins are. That's where I'd want to start."

That is the call to action. Not a retainer. Not a timeline. A specific deliverable in a specific window. That's what turns a discovery call into a Round 2.
