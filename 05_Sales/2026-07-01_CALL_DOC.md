## Instagram (Reviewed 2026-06-29)

*Full analysis: TDG-Intel/tdg/sandbox/vts-ig-review/VTS_IG_ANALYSIS_2026-06-29.md*

**The gap in numbers:** 206K followers. Pop culture posts: 500-2,000 likes. Product posts: 40-100 likes. That is roughly a 10-to-1 engagement gap between cultural content and product content. Consistent across the sample.

**What drives engagement:**
- Pop culture + tactical identity overlap: John Wayne Father's Day (2,098 likes), Indiana Jones (501), Heat clip (791), Optimus Prime Prime Day meme (1,200), Predator meme (977). The audience shows up for any content that says "we see you, we are you."
- Product + firearm featured together: The 1911 on the CCW sling bag got 1,145 likes. The gun is the trust signal — it tells the audience this is a carry brand, not cosplay.
- Copy-led cultural moments: "Tactical Dad. Civilian Mission." Father's Day post worked without any product because the language understood the audience.

**What does not drive engagement:**
- Sale/promotional posts: completely flat. The Independence Day 25% off sitewide posts were the lowest-performing content in the sample (15-28 likes). The audience does not engage with discount content.
- USA 250 Limited Edition Collection announcement: 28 likes. In the same week the Father's Day meme got 1,294. Well-executed photography and copy — but structured as an announcement, not as an invitation into identity.
- Standalone product lifestyle posts: messenger bag (40 likes), duffel (59 likes). Real products, good copy, wrong visual language.

**The persona this reveals:** Male, 35-50, grew up on 80s action movies, carries daily, has taste, resists overt gear-bro cosplay. Range on Saturday, golf on Sunday. Watched Heat more than once. Price is not the driver — these posts do not respond to discounts. They will pay full price for gear that matches their identity.

**The conversion mechanism is broken:** Posts point to WWW.VIKTOS.COM in caption text. No Instagram Shopping product tags visible. No story swipe-up to PDP in this sample. A post with 1,145 likes on a specific product, with no direct path to that product's page, converts almost nothing. Every step between Instagram and the cart is a drop-off.

**Frame for the call:**
> "You have 206,000 followers and some posts get 2,000 likes. Your product posts get 40. That gap is a strategy problem, not a content execution problem. Your audience has told you exactly what they respond to. The fix starts with defining the persona formally so every product post speaks to that person — not just to the product."

**For Ryan specifically (IT frame):**
> "Instagram Shopping tags let you tag the product directly in the image. Tap the product, go straight to the PDP. No homepage, no navigation. On a post that already gets strong engagement, that one change moves the conversion needle."

**What NOT to say:** "Your Instagram is bad." It is not bad. The brand voice is genuinely strong and differentiated — "Stay Rad. Stay Classy." does not exist anywhere else in the tactical space. The failure is specific: the bridge between cultural content and product content is missing, and the path from social to purchase is broken.
